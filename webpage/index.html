Predicting Breast Cancer Survival Rate

About our product

With the use of a dataset from NKI, our team worked together on a product that helps estimate the survival rate of a patient. This is done with the help of things such as SVM's and random forest classification. In addition, our product also recommends treatment plans based on those factors.

Data Exploration

Here you will find various visualizations that pertain to our dataset. These graphs explore things like the relationship between time recurrence and survival rate, treatment types to survival, and age to treatment, just to name a few.


This graph explores the relationship between time recurrence and a patient's survival rate. The x-axis is time recurrence and the y-axis is survival. This graph shows that there's a positive correlation between time recurrence and a patient's survival rate.



This graph explores the relationships between survival, time recurrence, and age. With this, it shows that there's a positive correlation between some of those variables.

Linear Regression

Linear Regression is a system to predict and model the relationship between multiple variables. In our data set, we used linear regression to predict the relationship between survival and time recurrence. Using this, the mean average value (MAE) was calculated to find the average value of these data points and analyze the variability of data. Our MAE was about 8.5559.




SVM




Here we have a confusion matrix which is a type of support vector machine. A confusion matrix is a table that is used to define and display the performance of a classification algorithm. For this matrix, the x-values were age and time recurrence, and the target variable, which is the variable we were trying to predict, was survival rate. Overall, this matrix shows that after training the algorithm it was able to have an accuracy of 80% because out of the 55 values given, it was able to predict 44 correctly.
